Into the Guts of our Wiring: The thought behind the connections

The aim of the wiring is to connect the arduino to the steppers, limit switch, and E-stop. In addition, we connected the steppers to external voltage.

As far as the steppers go, we have the steppers connected to the drivers. The drivers then have a step wire and a direction wire that go to the arduino. The step wire lets the stepper know when to step while the direction pin determines the direction of the step. The arduino canâ€™t supply the 9 volt power needed to run the steppers, so the drivers are also connected to external batteries. Finally, the drivers are connected to 5 volts and ground from the arduino to power the logic.

The E-stop and limit switch are wired very similarly. They are both connected to a digital arduino pin set to high voltage and ground. When the button or switch is hit, its internal circuit is opened and the pin it is connected to no longer reads a high input.

Stepper motors contain a ring of wire coils. Every other coil is wired to the same current source. This means that current can be passed through the even coils one direction, while it is passed through the odd coils in the opposite direction. This causes magnetic fields which push and pull at the magnets attached to the shaft of the motor. When the current directions are switched back and forth, the stepper magnets are constantly being pulled towards the next coil in succession. This results in rotation.
